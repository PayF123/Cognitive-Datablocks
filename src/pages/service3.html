Mixed Reality

Mixed Reality (MR) is a hybrid technology that combines elements of both Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR). MR allows digital and physical objects to coexist and interact in real-time, creating a seamless blend of the real and virtual worlds. MR is used in advanced simulations, design, and collaborative workspaces.

  • Interactive Holograms
  • Spatial Computing

Interactive Holograms

  • Digital-Physical Interaction: MR enables digital objects (e.g., holograms) to interact with the real world and vice versa, creating a more immersive experience.
  • Use Cases: Design and engineering (e.g., 3D modeling), medical training (e.g., holographic anatomy models), and entertainment (e.g., interactive performances).

Spatial Computing

  • Real-World as a Canvas: MR uses the physical space around the user as a foundation for digital content, allowing for more natural interactions.
  • Use Cases: Architecture (e.g., virtual building walkthroughs), interior design (e.g., virtual furniture placement), and collaborative workspaces (e.g., virtual whiteboards).
