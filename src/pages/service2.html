Virtual Reality

Virtual Reality (VR) is a fully immersive technology that transports users into a completely digital environment. By wearing VR headsets, users can interact with and explore virtual worlds as if they were physically present. VR is widely used in gaming, training, education, and entertainment.

  • 360-Degree Videos
  • Immersive Environments
  • Social VR

360-Degree Videos

  • Immersive Video Experiences: VR allows users to watch 360-degree videos where they can look around in all directions, creating a sense of presence.
  • Use Cases: Entertainment (e.g., virtual concerts), journalism (e.g., immersive news stories), and education (e.g., virtual field trips).

Immersive Environments

  • Fully Digital Worlds: VR creates entirely virtual environments that users can explore and interact with, often using motion controllers or hand-tracking technology.
  • Use Cases: Gaming (e.g., VR games like Beat Saber), virtual tours (e.g., real estate or travel), and training simulations (e.g., flight simulators).

Social VR

  • Virtual Social Interactions: VR platforms enable users to meet, interact, and collaborate in virtual spaces, often represented by avatars.
  • Use Cases: Virtual meetings, conferences, and social networking (e.g., VRChat, AltspaceVR).
