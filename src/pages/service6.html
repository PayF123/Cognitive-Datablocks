Computer Vision

Computer Vision is a field of AI that enables machines to interpret and make decisions based on visual data from the world, such as images and videos. It mimics human vision to analyze and understand visual information.

  • Image Recognition
  • Video Analysis
  • Image Segmentation

Image Recognition

  • Object Detection: Identifies and locates objects within an image or video, used in surveillance, autonomous vehicles, and retail.
  • Facial Recognition: Detects and identifies human faces, used in security, authentication, and social media tagging.

Video Analysis

  • Motion Detection: Identifies movement within video footage, useful in security and surveillance.
  • Activity Recognition: Recognizes specific actions or activities in video streams, used in sports analytics and healthcare.

Image Segmentation

  • Semantic Segmentation: Labels each pixel in an image with a corresponding class, used in medical imaging and autonomous driving.
  • Instance Segmentation: Differentiates between different instances of the same object class, useful in robotics and augmented reality.
