Augmented Reality

Augmented Reality (AR) is a technology that overlays digital content—such as images, videos, or 3D models—onto the real world. Unlike Virtual Reality (VR), which creates a fully immersive digital environment, AR enhances the real world by adding virtual elements that users can interact with in real-time. AR is widely used in gaming, retail, education, and industrial applications.

  • Object Overlay
  • Location-Based AR
  • Marker-Based AR

Object Overlay

  • Digital Objects in Real World: AR superimposes digital objects (e.g., characters, information, or animations) onto the physical environment, visible through devices like smartphones, tablets, or AR glasses.
  • Use Cases: retail (e.g., virtual try-ons for clothes or furniture), and education (e.g., interactive learning tools).

Location-Based AR

  • Geolocation Integration: AR applications use GPS, compass, and other sensors to provide context-aware digital content based on the user's location.
  • Use Cases: Navigation aids (e.g., AR maps), tourism (e.g., historical information overlaid on landmarks), and location-based gaming.

Marker-Based AR

  • Triggered by Visual Markers: AR content is activated when a device recognizes specific visual markers, such as QR codes, images, or objects.
  • Use Cases: Advertising (e.g., interactive print media), product packaging (e.g., additional product information), and education (e.g., AR-enhanced textbooks).
